% Career Synopsis
\section*{Career Synopsis}
João Pedro Azevedo is UNICEF’s first Chief Statistician, appointed in February 2023 at UNICEF Headquarters to lead the organization’s global data and statistical work. He brings more than 15 years of experience in international development and five years in the Brazilian public sector, both at the federal and state levels. Previously, he worked at the World Bank as Lead Economist and EdTech Fellow, coordinating global initiatives on education and poverty. He holds a PhD in Economics from Newcastle University, and his research has been published in top journals like \textit{Lancet} and \textit{World Development}.

% Work Experience
\section{Work Experience}

\cventry{March 2023 -- Present}{Chief Statistician \& Deputy Director}{UNICEF Headquarters}{New York, USA}{}{
João Pedro Azevedo is UNICEF’s first Chief Statistician, appointed in February 2023 at the organization’s headquarters in New York. In this role, he leads UNICEF’s global data and statistical work, focusing on harnessing the transformative power of data to achieve results for children. He also holds the role of Deputy Director, responsible for a section that comprises ten units, including Population, Education, Poverty, Equity and Gender, Nutrition, Health, WASH, Child Protection, Climate, Frontier Data, and Data Collection/Survey Methodology. With a team of 45 international staff and an annual budget of approximately \$20 million, Mr. Azevedo oversees the quality, timely production, and delivery of over 300 child-related indicators, including 19 Sustainable Development Indicators (SDGs). He also provides country-level technical assistance on child-centered household surveys, such as the Multiple Indicators Cluster Survey, and ensures the production of around 50 publications per year, including refereed journal articles and UNICEF’s flagship reports on the progress of child-related SDGs.
}

\cventry{October 2018 -- February 2023}{Lead Economist \& EdTech Fellow}{World Bank Education Practice}{Washington, D.C., USA}{}{
João Pedro Azevedo served as a Lead Economist and EdTech Fellow at the World Bank’s Education Global Practice. He was responsible for scaling the measurement and operationalization of educational outcomes as part of the World Bank’s Human Capital Project. A core member in creating the World Bank Learning Poverty measure and global target, he worked on harmonizing over 1,000 learning assessments. He also led the EduAnalytics team and helped develop protocols for global learning assessments, which included ensuring the reproducibility of indicator work.
}

\cventry{June 2015 -- September 2018}{Lead Economist}{World Bank Poverty and Equity Global Practice}{Washington, D.C., USA}{}{
As Lead Economist at the World Bank’s Poverty and Equity Global Practice, João Pedro Azevedo led the Global Solution Group on Welfare Measurement and Statistical Capacity for Results. He was instrumental in the development of the Data for Goals agenda and oversaw the global harmonization of 1,200 household surveys for poverty monitoring. His work also included leading the joint poverty mapping project with the European Union and implementing the Croatian Index of Multiple Deprivation.
}

\cventry{February 2013 -- June 2015}{Senior Economist}{World Bank Poverty and Equity Practice}{Europe and Central Asia}{}{
João Pedro Azevedo led the World Bank’s knowledge and advisory work on poverty, equity, and shared prosperity for the Government of Turkey. He also co-led the World Bank’s advisory services in Central Asia, engaging in active policy dialogues with Tajikistan, Kazakhstan, and Uzbekistan. As head of the ECA Statistical Team, he provided critical insights into welfare and poverty dynamics for the region.
}

\cventry{August 2011 -- January 2013}{Senior Economist}{World Bank PREM/LAC}{Colombia}{}{
During his tenure as Senior Economist at the World Bank’s PREM/LAC department, João Pedro Azevedo led the organization’s knowledge and advisory work on poverty, equity, and monitoring and evaluation for the Government of Colombia. He was responsible for the development and leadership of the LAC Statistical Team and the LAC Monitoring and Evaluation Network, which helped guide policy decisions in the region.
}

\cventry{February 2007 -- January 2008}{Superintendent for Public Policy Evaluation}{Treasury of the State of Rio de Janeiro}{Brazil}{}{
As Superintendent for Public Policy Evaluation, João Pedro Azevedo led a team of econometricians that provided technical support to the State Treasury of Rio de Janeiro. His key projects included creating indicators to evaluate the state's fiscal policies and business environment, evaluating the efficiency of state auditors, and conducting an econometric analysis of the effectiveness of federal tax policies aimed at supporting micro-entrepreneurs.
}

\cventry{October 2004 -- December 2007}{Executive Coordinator, National Evaluation of the Programme Brasil Alfabetizado}{Brazilian Ministry of Education / UNESCO / UNDP}{Brazil}{}{
João Pedro Azevedo was responsible for the national evaluation of the Brazilian adult literacy program, "Brasil Alfabetizado." His work included designing the evaluation framework, implementing a panel survey of program beneficiaries, and providing technical assistance on instrument design, sample development, and data analysis. His findings offered critical insights into the program's targeting, efficiency, and overall effectiveness, influencing policy recommendations for future iterations of the literacy program.
}
